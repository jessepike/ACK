**When:** You're stuck on a section

**Start with:**
> "I'm stuck on the [section name] section of [artifact]. The section

## üí° TIPS FOR EFFECTIVE SESSIONS

### Be Specific
**Good:** "Can you review my 'Core Features' section for clarity?"
**Bad:** "Is this good?"

### Show Your Work
**Good:** "Here's my draft: [paste content]. I'm unsure about..."
**Bad:** "Can you write this for me?"

### Ask for Options
**Good:** "Should this be prose or bullets? Show me both?"
**Bad:** "Which is better?" (without showing options)

### State Your Stage
**Good:** "I'm in Week 1, Day 3 - working on research.md"
**Bad:** No context about where you are

---

## üö´ AVOID SESSION DRIFT

### Stay Focused
- **Each session = One clear goal**
- Finish one artifact before switching
- Don't jump between weeks randomly

### Prevent Scope Creep
- Week 1 = Writing only (no coding)
- Week 2 = Building only (no redesigning)
- Save "wouldn't it be cool if..." for later

### Know When to Stop
- Good stopping point: Artifact section complete
- Bad stopping point: Mid-thought, mid-section
- Always end with clear "next step"

## ‚ö° MOMENTUM KILLERS TO AVOID

### 1. Perfectionism
**Problem:** Endlessly editing the same section  
**Solution:** "Good enough, move on" - refine later

### 2. Analysis Paralysis
**Problem:** Researching competitors for 8 hours  
**Solution:** Set timer (2 hours max), then write

### 3. Scope Expansion
**Problem:** "We should also build X, Y, Z..."  
**Solution:** Add to "Future Ideas" section, not MVP

### 4. Tool Obsession
**Problem:** Spending days setting up perfect workflow  
**Solution:** Plain markdown files are fine for Week 1

### 5. Isolation
**Problem:** Writing in vacuum, no feedback  
**Solution:** Share drafts with friends, get reactions
